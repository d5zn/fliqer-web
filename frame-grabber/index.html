What Is a Frame Grabber?

A frame grabber is a tool that captures a single still image from a video at any chosen moment. Instead of taking a screenshot of your screen, a frame grabber extracts the actual video frame in its original resolution — pixel-perfect, with no compression artifacts from screen recording.

Whether you need to grab a frame for a thumbnail, reference shot, motion analysis, or storyboard — Fliqer gives you frame-accurate control down to the exact millisecond, right in your browser.

How to Grab a Frame from Video

  1. Drop or select your video file — MP4, MOV, WebM supported
  2. Use the timeline or step controls to find the exact frame
  3. Click Capture to grab the frame instantly
  4. Download it as full-resolution PNG or JPEG — done

FAQ

What is a frame grabber?

A frame grabber is a tool that captures a single still image from a video at any chosen moment. It lets you grab a frame from video and save it as a full-resolution photo — no video editing software needed.

How do I use Fliqer as an online frame grabber?

Open the Fliqer frame grabber in your browser, drop in your video, scrub to the exact frame you want, and click Capture. The image downloads instantly in full resolution. No upload, no account required.

Can I grab multiple frames at once?

Yes. Use the step controls to move through the video frame by frame or in larger steps (3, 5, 10, 30 frames) and capture as many frames as you need.

Does the frame grabber work without uploading my video?

Yes. Fliqer processes everything locally inside your browser. Your video is never uploaded to any server, so your files stay completely private.

What formats does the frame grabber export?

The online frame grabber exports frames as PNG or JPEG. The iOS app also supports HEIC for the most efficient file sizes.

Is this frame grabber free?

Yes. The Fliqer online frame grabber is completely free to use in your browser with no signup required. The iOS app also includes a free tier with 30 credits per month.
